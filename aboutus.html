About Bidhan Chandra College

Bidhan Chandra College, Rishra, affiliated with the University of Calcutta, is located in the heart of the industrial belt in the Serampore Sub-Division of Hooghly District, West Bengal. Established in 1957 and named after the eminent Dr. Bidhan Chandra Roy, the college was founded with the support of the Birkmyre Education Trust and contributions from dedicated individuals like Late Shribhusan Bose, Late Pannalal Mukherjee, and Late Principal Kanai Lal Ganguly.

The serene college campus offers a refreshing escape from the noise of G.T. Road, with a picturesque view of the holy Bhagirathi River and lush greenery along its strand. The diverse student community, comprising various castes, communities, and cultural backgrounds, fosters a broad, inclusive, and vibrant campus life.

Notably, the college attracts a significant number of first-generation learners—many of whom have gone on to achieve remarkable success in their careers.

Aim & Objective

The primary aim of the institution is to provide quality education while nurturing habits of discipline, self-reliance, and accountability among students. The objective is to refine both academic and co-curricular talents, preparing students for a dynamic and unpredictable future.

Mission & Motto

Our mission is to promote an integrated and harmonious personality among students from all walks of life, fostering a cosmopolitan and inclusive spirit. The college emblem, depicting an earthen flaming torch on a dark background, symbolizes our motto: “Tamaso Maa Jyotirgamaya” — From darkness to light.
